JFK Terminal-by-Terminal Pickup Guide

John F. Kennedy International consists of multiple terminals, each with distinct layouts, airline assignments, and pickup procedures. Understanding where you're landing and where to meet your chauffeur eliminates confusion and delays. When you book with us, we'll know your terminal based on your airline and flight information, but here's what to expect at each one:

Terminal Navigation Tips

All terminals at JFK have designated arrivals areas where our chauffeurs wait with name signs. After deplaning and collecting your checked luggage from baggage claim, follow signs to "Ground Transportation" or "Arrivals." Your chauffeur will be positioned in a visible location within the arrivals hall, holding a sign with your name clearly displayed.

Before your flight lands, you'll receive a text message with your chauffeur's name, photo, vehicle description, and direct phone number. This allows you to contact them directly if you need assistance finding each other. Our chauffeurs also monitor flight status continuously, so they know if you're delayed and will adjust their positioning accordingly.

International Arrivals

International flights require additional time for customs and immigration clearance. We include 60 minutes of complimentary wait time for all international arrivals, starting from your actual landing time—not your scheduled time. This gives you ample time to clear immigration (which can take 20-45 minutes depending on how many flights land simultaneously), collect checked bags, pass through customs, and proceed to the arrivals area where your chauffeur is waiting.

During peak international arrival times (typically early morning and late afternoon), the customs halls can be particularly busy. Our 60-minute buffer accommodates this, and if you're running even later due to exceptional circumstances, just text your chauffeur to let them know. We don't charge extra for reasonable delays beyond your control.

Domestic Arrivals

Domestic flights generally move much faster. You deplane, collect any checked bags from baggage claim (if applicable), and proceed directly to ground transportation. We include 30 minutes of complimentary wait time for domestic arrivals, which is typically more than sufficient. Most passengers meet their chauffeur within 10-15 minutes of landing.

How it works: Open app after landing, request ride, wait for driver to accept and navigate to airport, meet at designated rideshare pickup area (often requiring shuttle bus or long walk), hope for decent vehicle.

Taxi

How it works: Join taxi queue at designated taxi stand, wait your turn (can be 5-30 minutes), take next available taxi.

Advantages: No reservation needed, fixed meter rates (usually), always available.

Disadvantages: Long waits during busy times, vehicle cleanliness varies dramatically, driver knowledge varies, no flight tracking or meet-and-greet, cramped with luggage, no child car seats, cash or card (card machines "broken" surprisingly often), gratuity expected but not included.

Best for: Spontaneous travelers who don't mind waiting and don't have heavy luggage.

Rental Car

How it works: Take rental car shuttle to off-airport lot, wait in line, complete paperwork, inspect and accept vehicle, navigate unfamiliar area to destination, deal with parking.

Advantages: Have vehicle for duration of trip, can be economical for longer stays.

Disadvantages: Time-consuming pickup process (30-60 minutes), navigation in unfamiliar city, parking costs and hassles in New York, insurance decisions, fuel requirements, return process, not productive travel time (you're driving).

Best for: Multi-day trips where you'll need a car throughout your stay.

Cost: $40-$120/day plus gas, tolls, parking, insurance.

Airport Shuttle/Shared Van

How it works: Book shared shuttle, van makes multiple stops at different hotels/addresses, wait for other passengers, extended travel time.

Advantages: Economical for solo travelers.

Disadvantages: Multiple stops add 30-90 minutes to journey, rigid schedules, share space with strangers, limited luggage capacity, cannot deviate from scheduled stops.

Best for: Solo budget travelers with lots of time and going to a major hotel.

Cost: $25-$45 per person, but takes 2-3x longer than direct service.

Public Transportation

How it works: Navigate to train/bus station in airport, purchase tickets, manage luggage on crowded transit, transfer to connecting lines, walk from final stop to destination.

Advantages: Very economical, operates on frequent schedules.

Disadvantages: Difficult with luggage, time-consuming with connections and waits, requires familiarity with system, uncomfortable with bags and fellow passengers, limited hours on some lines, not door-to-door, impossible for families with young children and luggage.

Best for: Solo budget travelers with backpack only who know the transit system.

Cost: $5-$20, but requires significant time and effort.

Tips for First-Time Travelers to JFK

If this is your first time flying through John F. Kennedy International, here's what you should know to make your experience smooth:

Before Your Trip

  • Book car service in advance: Don't wait until you land and hope for the best with rideshare. Book at least 24-48 hours ahead for guaranteed availability and the best rates.
  • Provide your flight details: When booking, give us your airline and flight number. This lets us track your flight automatically and know exactly which terminal you'll be in.
  • Save your confirmation: You'll receive confirmation via email and text with your chauffeur's contact info. Save this or keep it easily accessible on your phone.
  • Know your terminal: Different airlines use different terminals at JFK. Your boarding pass will show your terminal, or check your airline's website.
  • Have a backup plan: Save our phone number in your contacts. If something unexpected happens, you can call or text our dispatch team 24/7.

Upon Arrival

  • Turn on your phone: As soon as you land, turn off airplane mode. You'll receive a text from your chauffeur confirming they're positioned and ready.
  • Collect your luggage efficiently: If you checked bags, head directly to baggage claim. Don't stop for long restroom breaks or meals before getting bags—your chauffeur is waiting. Grab your bags, then take care of other needs if necessary.
  • Follow ground transportation signs: Every terminal has clear signage to "Ground Transportation" or "Arrivals." Follow these signs to reach the area where your chauffeur is waiting.
  • Look for your name sign: Your chauffeur will be holding a professional sign with your name clearly displayed. They're usually near the main exit or in a central location visible from multiple angles.
  • If you can't find your chauffeur: Don't panic. Call or text the number from your confirmation. They'll guide you to their exact position. Sometimes you're looking at different exits or they've been asked to reposition by airport staff.
  • Let your chauffeur handle luggage: That's what they're there for. They'll assist with your bags to the vehicle.

During the Ride

  • Confirm your destination: When you get in the vehicle, confirm your destination address with the chauffeur. This ensures you're both on the same page.
  • Work or relax: Use the WiFi if you need to work or check email. Or just relax—you're not driving, you don't need to navigate, you can decompress from your flight.
  • Ask questions: Our chauffeurs know New York intimately. If you're visiting and want restaurant recommendations, directions to attractions, or local insights, just ask.
  • No cash needed: Everything is pre-paid through your booking. No fumbling for cash, no worrying about having small bills for tips. Just enjoy the ride.

For Departures

  • Confirm pickup time: When you book, we'll suggest optimal pickup time based on your flight. If you want to adjust, just let us know.
  • Be ready: Your chauffeur will arrive about 5 minutes before your scheduled pickup. Being ready to go avoids delays.
  • Know your terminal and airline: For departures, make sure you've told us your airline. We'll drop you at the correct terminal entrance.
  • Plan for traffic: Even though we build in appropriate buffer time, construction or accidents can happen. Booking pickups during off-peak hours when possible provides extra margin.
  • TSA PreCheck/Clear: If you have these, mention it when booking. We can adjust timing since you'll move through security faster.

Common requests include stopping at a office to drop off a colleague, stopping at a grocery store, or stopping at a restaurant for takeout. Just mention the stop when booking or ask your chauffeur during the trip. For multiple stops or complex itineraries, hourly service may be more economical than point-to-point with stop fees.

Can I track my ride or my chauffeur's location?

You'll receive text updates when your chauffeur is assigned and when they're positioned at the airport for pickup. For departures, you'll receive a text when they're en route to your pickup location. If you need real-time tracking or updates, you can call or text your chauffeur directly using the number provided in your confirmation.

Do you offer wheelchair accessible vehicles for JFK airport service?

Yes, we have wheelchair accessible vehicles with ramps or lifts. When booking, request an accessible vehicle and provide details about the wheelchair or mobility scooter (dimensions, weight) to ensure we assign the appropriate vehicle. These vehicles should be booked as far in advance as possible as we have limited accessible fleet compared to standard vehicles.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept all major credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover) and corporate accounts with approved billing. Payment is processed when you book, so there's no payment required at the time of service—your chauffeur won't ask for payment or handle money. Corporate accounts can be invoiced monthly with net-30 terms.
